Tournament History

A tradition of friendly rivalry, great golf, and lasting friendships since 2015.

2015

The inaugural Wyatt Cup was held at Riverside Golf Club. Town edged out Country by 2 points in a thrilling debut.

2017

Country claimed their first victory at Pine Valley, with a dominant performance on the back nine sealing the win.

2019

The closest finish yet — a single point separated the teams at Lakeside Country Club. Town prevailed in a playoff hole.

2021

Post-pandemic return at The Oaks. Record turnout with 16 players. Country won convincingly by 5 points.

2023

Town's revenge at Harbour Town. A masterclass in match play saw them reclaim the Cup by 4 points.

2024

Country struck back at Sunset Ridge. The largest crowd yet witnessed a dramatic Sunday comeback.

2026

The 10th edition introduces non-playing captains for the first time. The rivalry continues at a brand new venue.

Introducing Non-Playing Captains

A new chapter for the Wyatt Cup 2026.

New for 2026

For the first time in Wyatt Cup history, each team will have a non-playing captain. These captains won't swing a club on the course — instead, they'll lead from the sidelines, making strategic decisions, motivating their teams, and adding a whole new dimension to the rivalry. Think of it as the Ryder Cup meets your local club championship.

Richard Townsend

Non-Playing Captain
Town

A former county-level player and lifelong golf enthusiast, Richard brings tactical acumen and a calm demeanor. He's been involved with the Wyatt Cup since day one and knows every player's strengths and weaknesses.

Angus McAllister

Non-Playing Captain
Country

A charismatic leader with an infectious love of the game, Angus is known for his rousing team talks and clever pairings. His ability to read the momentum of a match is second to none.
